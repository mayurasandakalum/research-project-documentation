Introduction

Empowering Young Learners, Transforming Education in Sri Lanka

Mathematics is a core subject that builds logical thinking and problem-solving skills in young learners. However, many students in Sri Lanka — especially in Grade 4 and 5 — struggle to keep up due to traditional, one-size-fits-all teaching methods that fail to address individual learning styles.

විදුමග: AI-Enhanced Mathematics Learning for Grade 4 & 5 Sinhala Students is a research-based initiative aimed at transforming primary math education through Artificial Intelligence. This project leverages the VARK learning model (Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, Kinesthetic) to deliver personalized, multimodal content in Sinhala — the first language of the learners.

Motivated by the lack of inclusive and adaptive learning tools in resource-constrained environments, this platform was developed to empower students, support teachers, and bridge educational gaps using cutting-edge deep learning models.

Abstract

Innovating Math Education with AI for Sinhala Students

The විදුමග: AI-Enhanced Mathematics Learning for Grade 4 & 5 Sinhala Students project addresses the critical challenge of ineffective traditional math education for Sinhala-speaking primary students in Sri Lanka. Leveraging the VARK learning model (Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, Kinesthetic), this research develops an AI-driven platform that delivers personalized, adaptive math lessons tailored to individual learning styles.

Conducted through a mixed-methods approach, the study involved needs assessments, system design using deep learning technologies (e.g., TensorFlow, speech recognition), and pilot testing with 150 students across urban and rural schools. Results indicate a 25% improvement in math test scores and 85% student engagement, demonstrating the platform's potential to enhance learning outcomes in resource-constrained environments.

This initiative aims to empower students, support educators, and pave the way for scalable educational innovation.

Literature Survey

Exploring the Foundations of AI-Driven Education

The development of විදුමග: AI-Enhanced Mathematics Learning for Grade 4 & 5 Sinhala Students builds on extensive research in adaptive learning, AI in education, and culturally relevant pedagogy. Studies such as Fleming and Mills (1992) introduced the VARK model, highlighting the importance of tailoring education to Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, and Kinesthetic learning styles to improve student outcomes.

Recent advancements in AI, as explored by Chen et al. (2020), demonstrate the efficacy of deep learning models like speech recognition and handwriting detection in creating multimodal learning systems, particularly for primary education. In the Sri Lankan context, Perera and Abeysekera (2018) identified significant gaps in math education for Sinhala-medium students, noting the lack of personalized tools in resource-limited settings.

Additionally, research by Wijesekera (2023) emphasized the need for culturally relevant, language-specific educational platforms to enhance engagement among young learners. This project integrates these insights, combining AI technologies with the VARK framework to address the unique challenges faced by Grade 4 and 5 Sinhala students.

Research Gaps

Addressing Critical Needs in AI-Enhanced Education

The විදුමග: AI-Enhanced Mathematics Learning for Grade 4 & 5 Sinhala Students project targets specific gaps in existing research, outlined below as key points:

Limited Focus on Non-English Contexts

Studies like Chen et al. (2020) demonstrate AI's potential in education but primarily focus on Western, English-speaking environments, overlooking the linguistic and cultural needs of Sinhala-medium students in Sri Lanka.

Lack of Personalized Solutions

Research by Perera and Abeysekera (2018) highlights math education challenges for Grade 4 and 5 students in Sri Lanka, yet it fails to propose personalized learning solutions that cater to diverse learning styles in resource-limited settings.

Insufficient Language-Specific AI Tools

While Wijesekera (2023) stresses the need for language-specific educational tools, there is a lack of AI-integrated platforms designed for Sinhala-medium math curricula, limiting accessibility for young learners.

Underutilization of VARK in Local Contexts

The VARK model is widely recognized for tailoring education to individual learning styles, but its application in Sri Lankan primary education, particularly for Sinhala students, remains underexplored, leaving a gap in inclusive, multimodal learning systems.

Research Problem & Solution

Addressing Key Challenges with Innovative Approaches

Research Problem

Challenges in Mathematics Education for Sinhala Primary Students

Grade 4 and 5 Sinhala students in Sri Lanka face significant hurdles in mathematics due to traditional teaching methods that do not accommodate diverse learning styles, as identified by the VARK model (Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, Kinesthetic). These one-size-fits-all approaches often lead to disengagement and poor performance, particularly in resource-constrained environments.

Additionally, the lack of culturally relevant, language-specific tools in Sinhala limits accessibility and inclusivity, exacerbating educational gaps. Existing AI-driven educational solutions, while promising, are predominantly designed for English-speaking contexts, leaving a critical need for localized, adaptive learning systems tailored to Sinhala-medium primary education.

Solution

An AI-Driven, Personalized Learning Platform for Sinhala Students

විදුමග: AI-Enhanced Mathematics Learning for Grade 4 & 5 Sinhala Students addresses these challenges by introducing an innovative AI-powered platform that delivers personalized math education in Sinhala. Utilizing the VARK framework, the system adapts to each student's learning style through multimodal content—incorporating speech recognition for auditory learners, handwriting detection for kinesthetic learners, and visual aids for visual learners.

Designed for low-resource settings, විදුමග ensures accessibility with minimal internet and device requirements. It also provides teachers with real-time feedback tools to monitor progress, fostering an inclusive, engaging, and effective learning environment that bridges educational gaps for Sinhala primary students.

Data Collection & Processing

Our methodology included comprehensive data collection from Grade 4-5 math curricula, student learning patterns, and Sinhala language resources to train our AI models effectively.

AI Integration

We implemented neural networks for handwriting recognition, speech processing for audio commands, and adaptive learning algorithms to personalize each student's educational journey.

User Experience Design

We created culturally relevant interfaces with Sinhala typography and local visual elements, ensuring an intuitive experience for young learners with varied digital literacy levels.

Testing & Validation

We conducted extensive pilot testing with 150 students across urban and rural schools, collecting qualitative feedback and quantitative performance metrics to refine our approach.

Curriculum Integration

We aligned our content with the Sri Lankan National Curriculum for Grades 4-5, ensuring educational relevance while enhancing teaching methods through multimodal approaches.

VARK Implementation

We developed specialized algorithms that identify individual learning styles through usage patterns, automatically adjusting content delivery to match Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, or Kinesthetic preferences.
